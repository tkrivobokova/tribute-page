Johann Sebastian Bach

“Music is an agreeable harmony for the honor of God and the permissible delights of the soul.”
Nineteenth century engraving of portrait of Johann Sebastian Bach by August Weger (1823-1892) of Leizig
Short chronology:
  • 1685 - born in Eisenach
  • 1693 – 1695 - attends the Latin grammar school
  • 1695 - moves to Ohrdruf
  • 1702 - applies for the position of organist in Sangerhausen
  • 1703 - joines Duke Johann Ernst III’s private orchestra in Weimar
  • 1707 - becomes the organist at St Blasius’s Church in Mühlhausen
  • 1714 - is promoted to concertmaster
  • 1717 - signes contract as kapellmeister at the court of Prince Leopold of Anhalt-Köthen
  • 1749 - his health and vision declined
  • 1750 - dies at Leipzig at the age of 65
Bach’s deep religious faith could be found even in his secular music. He would put the initials “I.N.J.,” a Latin abbreviation that means, In Nomine Jesu, or “in the name of Jesus,”on his manuscripts.
